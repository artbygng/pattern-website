Aimed at parents who've heard "Montessori" as a buzzword and want to know if it actually applies here — and if it justifies the effort of making one.

## Outline
- What "Montessori-style" actually means for a toy: independence, real fine-motor skill building, natural materials, no batteries/screens
- How quiet book pages map to these principles (buttons, laces, zippers, counting, matching)
- Address the skepticism directly: "isn't this just a fancy toy?" — yes, but the design intent is different from a plastic equivalent
- Longevity angle: grows with the child vs. outgrown in a season
