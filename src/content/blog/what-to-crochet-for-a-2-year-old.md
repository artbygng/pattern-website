The short answer to what to crochet for a 2 year old is: something they can play with. Past the blanketand-booties stage, they love and develop through pretend play, sorting, stacking, and doing things with their hands. The best crochet gifts for this age are toys, and the good news for you as the crocheter: toys are small, fast, stash-friendly projects.

Here are ten ideas I hope you'll enjoy.

## 1. A bib

The nice quick win on this list, and a make that earns its keep for many meals. A bib for a two year old needs to be durable and resilient, it holds up to heavy food and constant washing — a sturdy cotton yarn and a secure neck will do the trick.

Most free bib patterns online stop at newborn sizing, so look for one that's actually built to grow. Make It with Meggie's [Apple Blossom Bib](https://makewithmeggie.com/2020/07/09/round-bib/) is a good one — the designer specifically addresses sizing it up for an older toddler by lengthening the neck ties.

## 2. A simple crochet ball

I absolutely love this next pattern for an African crochet ball. You can choose your own colors and make it as bright or pastel shaded as you like. Soft enough for indoor throwing, textured enough for little hands to grip, and washable. I've made it myself and it was a hit with the little ones playing with it. This [free African ball pattern](https://crochetbug.com/how-to-make-an-african-flower-soccer-ball/) by Crochetbug will explain how to create the hexagons, and there's a link to a page explaining how to put the ball together.

## 3. Finger puppets

Finger puppets are a great way to use your scrap-yarn. You won't need a lot of each color, and can mix and match with the different colors you have. Make as many as you like, create stories around the characters, and let your kids imagination run wild. Furls Crochet has a [free finger puppet pattern](https://furlscrochet.com/blogs/amigurumi-crochet-tutorials/free-crochet-finger-puppets-pattern) that's a good jumping-off point. Note that they are rather small so supervision is needed for younger kids.

## 4. A fuzzy amigurumi animal

Simple but classic - a bunny or bear never go out of style. For a 2-year-old, keep it simple and sturdy: chunky limbs, embroidered face (I'd recommend skipping plastic safety eyes at this age — stitch the eyes with yarn instead), and firm stuffing so it survives being loved hard. All About Ami's free [Boucle Bear & Bunny pattern](https://www.allaboutami.com/pattern-boucle-bear-bunny/) is a lovely place to start.

## 5. Stacking rings or nesting bowls

I've made a bunch of stacking ring toys myself, and they're always a lot of fun. Toddlers stack, sort, and un-nest them, and it's exactly the fine-motor practice this age is built for. Montessori-style toy, scrap-friendly, no small parts. Off the Beaten Hook has a [free stacking toy pattern and video tutorial](https://offthebeatenhook.com/crochet-stacking-toy/) that walks through the whole thing.

## 6. A pull-up vegetable garden

A little crochet "garden: carrots, radishes, and beets that tuck into pockets or a base and get "harvested. This make is pretend play and fine-motor work in one. It photographs adorably, too, if you like sharing your makes. Winding Road Crochet has a [free baby toy garden pattern](https://www.windingroadcrochet.com/crochet-baby-toy-container-garden/) with a carrot, radish, beet, and flower to pull and re-plant.

## 7. A little car or truck

For the vehicle-obsessed toddler (you know if you have one), a crochet car is soft enough for the crib and quiet on the floors. One Dog Woof has a [free amigurumi car pattern](https://www.1dogwoof.com/amigurumi-crochet-car-pattern/) in red, very cute and perfect for pretend play. For this one as well I'd recommend skipping plastic safety eyes and stitching the eyes with yarn instead.

## 8. A hat they'll really love

The one wearable that earns its place on a toy list: a character hat — a bear with ears, a strawberry, a dinosaur with spikes. Make the process of getting dressed fun! Relatively quick make, big cuteness payoff, and can be handed down once outgrown. Handy Little Me has a [roundup of 25 free animal hat patterns](https://www.handylittleme.com/animal-hat-crochet-patterns/) if you want options to pick from.

## 9. A hand puppet

A hand puppet turns you into the toy — instant puppet shows, silly voices, "the puppet says it's bathtime." Simple construction (a tube with a face and maybe ears), big daily-life payoff for the parents. Red Ted Art has a [free bunny hand puppet pattern](https://www.redtedart.com/bunny-hand-puppet-crochet-pattern/) that's an easy first one to try. If you choose the safety eyes, supervise the play at all times - while they're designed stay on securely, it's still important to be careful.

## 10. A crochet quiet book (the showstopper)

If you want the gift everyone remembers, crochet a quiet book. A soft book where every page has an activity — pieces to move, textures to feel, little scenarios to play out. It's the biggest project on this list, but the payoff is worth it. If you can make a bib or a stuffed animal, you can make a quiet book.

It's the gift that gets kept — played with for years, then saved in the keepsake box. I've written a [full beginner's guide to crocheting a quiet book](/blog/how-to-crochet-a-quiet-book-beginners-guide), and if you'd rather skip the design work, my quiet book patterns (like the bestselling [Bake Time](/patterns/bake-time)) walk you through every page with photos at every step.

## FAQ

### What's the best yarn for toddler toys?

Depending on the make, I'd recommend worsted-weight acrylic, cotton or bamboo yarn, an chunky yarn. chunky yarn is great for fluffy stuffed animals, cotton and bamboo can be used for makes with finer details, and acrylic yarn for interactive toys (cotton and bamboo will work for those as well, but might be more work because they're thinner). You can work with a hook one size smaller than the label suggests so stuffing stays hidden and pieces keep their structure.

### Can a beginner crochet a toy for a 2-year-old?

Yes — start with the simpler projects labeled for beginners, like a small stuffed animal or a simple hat.

### How long do these projects take?

The bib and finger puppets might take an evening each depending on your speed. A hat or small amigurumi will take a bit longer. A full quiet book can take a few weeks at a relaxed pace — see [how to crochet a quiet book](/blog/how-to-crochet-a-quiet-book-beginners-guide) for the full breakdown.

### Are crochet toys safe for a 2-year-old?

Well-made crochet toys are a great fit for this age — use tight stitches, firm stuffing, embroidered faces instead of plastic eyes, and no long cords. Always check finished toys for loose parts and supervise play, especially with removable pieces.

## The real answer and what I always opt for: make an interactive toy

I always find it more interesting for myselft to crochet toys and interactive pieces rather than clothes or blankets. I also find these are the kinds of gifts that make toddlers very happy. Start small with a bib or a ball, and when you're ready for the gift that becomes a family keepsake, the quiet book is waiting for you.
