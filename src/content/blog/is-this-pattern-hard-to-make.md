Targets the single biggest purchase-blocking worry: "am I actually skilled enough for this?" Should de-mystify the skill-level label rather than just restate it.

## Outline
- What "intermediate" means in practice here: comfort with basic stitches + willingness to follow a long multi-part pattern, not mastery of advanced techniques
- The self-check: if you've finished any multi-session crochet project before, you likely have what it takes
- What actually trips people up (per pattern skill notes: shaping, multi-piece assembly) vs. what doesn't (most of the yardage is repetitive single crochet)
- Reassurance: patterns are written to build skill progressively, page to page
- CTA: link to a specific pattern's skill-level breakdown section as an example
